Strategic Partnership with Fivetran

Announced September 1, 2025

We're excited to announce our strategic partnership with Fivetran, which makes Marketing Mix Modeling much easier by removing the data preparation bottleneck.

This Integration Makes It Much Easier

Fivetran's ad_reporting dbt package brings together spend, impressions, clicks, and conversion metrics from major channels (Google, Meta, LinkedIn, TikTok, Amazon) into a unified format. PyMC-Marketing now turns those standardized tables into production-grade Bayesian MMM models.

What We Shipped: New data loaders including process_fivetran_ad_reporting() and process_fivetran_shopify_unique_orders() that transform Fivetran/dbt outputs into MMM-ready inputs in just a few lines of code.

The Impact: Teams report moving from weeks of data wrangling to days for complete MMM deployment. This partnership enables data teams to focus on insights and decision-making rather than ETL complexity.

Who Benefits: In-house data teams using Fivetran, analytics consultancies serving multiple clients, and finance/growth leaders needing defendable ROAS and budget recommendations.

PyMC-Marketing vs. Google Meridian: Head-to-Head Comparison

Research Published September 8, 2025

Our research team published a detailed comparison between PyMC-Marketing and Google's Meridian, two popular open-source Marketing Mix Modeling libraries. We tested both tools with realistic datasets ranging from startup to enterprise scale.

Key Research Findings

  • Speed: PyMC-Marketing runs 2-20x faster than Meridian
  • Accuracy: PyMC-Marketing cuts modeling errors in half
  • Scale: Only PyMC-Marketing handled enterprise-level datasets successfully
  • Reliability: Better channel contribution estimates for budget decisions

The Study: We tested four dataset sizes from single-market startups to global enterprises like Nike or Colgate. Both tools used identical settings for a fair comparison, and all benchmark code is available on GitHub for transparency.

Research Team: Led by Teemu Säilynoja, Luca Fiaschi, and Jake Piekarski, this study provides the first rigorous technical comparison between these two widely-used MMM libraries.
